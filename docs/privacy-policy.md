Privacy Policy for NotifyBridge

Effective date: July 11, 2026
Last updated: July 11, 2026

NotifyBridge is developed and maintained by Mahmut Alperen Ünal (“Developer,” “we,” “us,” or “our”).

This Privacy Policy explains how the NotifyBridge Android application and its companion application for macOS access, process, transmit, store, and protect information.

NotifyBridge is designed as a privacy-focused, local-network utility. Its primary function is to forward notifications selected by the user from an Android device to a paired Mac over the user’s local network.

NotifyBridge does not operate a cloud service for notification delivery. Notification content is not transmitted to servers owned or controlled by the Developer.

1. Scope of this Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to:

* the NotifyBridge application for Android;
* the official NotifyBridge companion application for macOS;
* the local communication established between paired Android and macOS devices; and
* the NotifyBridge privacy policy page hosted through GitHub Pages.

This Privacy Policy does not govern third-party applications that generate notifications, the Android operating system, macOS, Google Play, GitHub, internet service providers, local network operators, or other services that are not controlled by the Developer.

2. Core Privacy Principles

NotifyBridge is designed according to the following principles:

* notification forwarding occurs directly between the user’s paired devices;
* notification content is not routed through a Developer-operated cloud server;
* no NotifyBridge account is required;
* no advertising SDK is included;
* no behavioral analytics SDK is included;
* no user profiling is performed;
* personal information is not sold;
* personal information is not used for advertising;
* personal information is not shared with data brokers; and
* pairing and configuration information is stored locally on the user’s devices.

3. Information Accessed and Processed by NotifyBridge

3.1 Android notification information

When the user voluntarily grants Android Notification Access, NotifyBridge may access and process information contained in notifications generated by applications installed on the Android device.

Depending on the originating application and the structure of a notification, this information may include:

* the name of the application that generated the notification;
* the application’s package identifier;
* the notification title;
* the notification text or message content;
* expanded notification content;
* notification timestamps;
* notification category and status;
* notification icons or other notification-related visual information;
* conversation or sender information included by the originating application;
* available notification actions;
* reply actions supported by the notification;
* notification grouping information; and
* identifiers required to update, dismiss, or associate a forwarded notification.

Notification content can contain personal or sensitive information depending on the applications used by the user. For example, notifications may contain private messages, verification codes, transaction alerts, calendar information, delivery updates, health-related information, or other content.

NotifyBridge does not determine or control the contents of notifications generated by third-party applications.

3.2 User-selected application settings

NotifyBridge may locally process and store user preferences such as:

* applications selected or excluded for notification forwarding;
* notification forwarding preferences;
* connection preferences;
* paired-device preferences;
* service state;
* permission status;
* user interface preferences; and
* other application configuration settings.

These settings are used only to provide and maintain the functionality requested by the user.

3.3 Paired-device and local-network information

To discover, pair with, authenticate, and communicate with a Mac, NotifyBridge may process technical information such as:

* local IP addresses;
* local network host information;
* port information;
* device names chosen or exposed during pairing;
* device or installation identifiers created for NotifyBridge;
* pairing tokens or pairing credentials;
* cryptographic keys or key-related material;
* certificate information;
* certificate fingerprints;
* connection status;
* protocol version information;
* timestamps;
* retry or reconnection state; and
* other technical information required to maintain a secure local connection.

This information is used to establish and protect communication between the user’s paired devices.

The Developer does not receive this local-network or pairing information through a NotifyBridge-operated server.

3.4 QR code and camera access

NotifyBridge may request access to the Android device’s camera when the user chooses to scan a pairing QR code displayed by the macOS companion application.

Camera access is used for QR-code scanning and pairing.

NotifyBridge does not use the camera for advertising, analytics, profiling, facial recognition, biometric identification, or background surveillance.

Camera images and video are not uploaded to the Developer. NotifyBridge does not intentionally retain photographs or video recordings captured during QR-code scanning.

Camera permission can be denied or revoked through Android system settings.

3.5 Notification permission

NotifyBridge may request permission to display its own Android notifications.

This permission may be used to:

* display the status of an active foreground service;
* inform the user about connection status;
* communicate errors or service conditions; and
* provide controls related to NotifyBridge operation.

This permission does not itself grant access to notifications created by other applications. Access to notifications from other applications is controlled separately through Android’s Notification Access settings.

3.6 Foreground service and background operation

NotifyBridge may operate a foreground service to maintain the user-requested notification forwarding connection.

The foreground service may display an ongoing system notification so that the user is aware that NotifyBridge is active.

This service is used only to provide the core notification-forwarding functionality and maintain connection reliability.

3.7 Battery optimization access

NotifyBridge may provide the user with an option to exempt the application from Android battery optimizations.

This is used to improve connection continuity and notification-forwarding reliability when Android or a device manufacturer restricts background activity.

NotifyBridge does not use this access for advertising, tracking, profiling, or unrelated background processing.

The user controls this setting through Android system settings and may revoke the exemption at any time.

3.8 Device restart information

If enabled by the user and permitted by the operating system, NotifyBridge may restart or restore its service state after the Android device restarts.

This behavior is used to restore previously selected notification-forwarding functionality. It is not used to transmit information to the Developer.

4. How Notification Information Is Used

Notification information accessed by NotifyBridge is used only to provide features requested by the user, including:

* forwarding eligible Android notifications to a paired Mac;
* displaying Android notifications through the macOS companion application;
* keeping forwarded notifications synchronized where supported;
* processing notification dismissal or removal events;
* presenting notification actions on the paired Mac;
* sending user-entered direct replies to the relevant Android notification where supported;
* opening or invoking supported notification actions;
* applying application and notification filters selected by the user; and
* maintaining the reliability and security of the local connection.

NotifyBridge does not use notification information to:

* create advertising profiles;
* personalize advertisements;
* measure advertising performance;
* perform behavioral analytics;
* sell personal information;
* conduct market research;
* train artificial intelligence models;
* build databases of notification content;
* identify the user’s interests;
* track the user across applications or websites; or
* provide data to data brokers.

5. Local Device-to-Device Transmission

NotifyBridge transmits notification information from the Android device to a Mac that the user has intentionally paired with the application.

This transmission is:

* initiated and controlled by the user;
* performed to provide NotifyBridge’s core functionality;
* directed to the user’s paired Mac;
* carried over the user’s local network; and
* protected using the security mechanisms implemented by NotifyBridge.

Notification information is not routed through a notification relay, analytics server, advertising server, or cloud storage service operated by the Developer.

The paired Mac is a separate device selected by the user. Anyone who can access that Mac, its user account, its screen, or its notification history may be able to view forwarded notification information. Users are responsible for protecting access to their paired devices and local network.

6. Notification Actions and Direct Replies

Where supported by Android and the originating application, NotifyBridge may allow the user to interact with a notification from the paired Mac.

This may include:

* dismissing a notification;
* activating an available notification action; or
* entering and sending a direct reply.

When the user submits a direct reply from the Mac:

1. the reply is transmitted over the local connection to the paired Android device;
2. NotifyBridge provides the reply to the relevant Android notification action; and
3. the originating third-party application may then process or transmit the reply according to that application’s own privacy policy and service architecture.

NotifyBridge does not control how the originating application processes a reply after it has been submitted to that application.

7. Data Collected by the Developer

The Developer does not operate a NotifyBridge backend that collects notification content, pairing credentials, direct replies, or application-filter selections.

NotifyBridge does not require the user to create an account or submit a name, telephone number, postal address, or other profile information to the Developer.

The Android and macOS applications do not intentionally transmit notification content to the Developer.

Because NotifyBridge is open-source software, independently modified builds, forks, or unofficial distributions may behave differently. This Privacy Policy applies only to the official NotifyBridge applications distributed or published by Mahmut Alperen Ünal.

8. Data Sharing

The Developer does not sell, rent, license, or disclose notification content to advertisers, data brokers, analytics providers, or other companies.

NotifyBridge does not share personal or sensitive information with third parties for:

* advertising;
* retargeting;
* profiling;
* creditworthiness decisions;
* insurance decisions;
* employment decisions;
* data brokerage;
* marketing; or

Notification information is transferred only to a Mac deliberately paired by the user as part of the application’s primary and user-visible functionality.

Information may also be processed by the operating systems, networks, application stores, source-code hosting providers, or third-party applications involved in the user’s environment. Such processing is controlled by those third parties and is governed by their respective terms and privacy policies.

9. Third-Party Applications

NotifyBridge processes notifications created by third-party Android applications.

Each third-party application is independently responsible for:

* the notification content it generates;
* how it obtains that content;
* how it processes user information;
* how it handles direct replies or actions; and
* its own privacy and security practices.

NotifyBridge is not responsible for the privacy practices of applications whose notifications are forwarded.

Users should review the privacy policies of those applications separately.

10. Data Storage

10.1 Android device

NotifyBridge may store the following information locally on the Android device:

* pairing configuration;
* paired-device records;
* connection settings;
* application-filter preferences;
* service preferences;
* technical state required for reconnection;
* cryptographic material protected through available Android security mechanisms; and
* other settings needed for application operation.

NotifyBridge is not designed to maintain a Developer-accessible cloud archive of notification content.

Some temporary notification information may exist in memory or local application state for as long as necessary to forward, update, synchronize, or act upon a notification.

10.2 macOS device

The macOS companion application may temporarily process or locally retain information required to display and manage forwarded notifications.

Notification information displayed by macOS may also be subject to the behavior and settings of macOS Notification Center.

The Developer does not remotely access the user’s macOS notification history.

10.3 Operating-system records

Android, macOS, network equipment, security software, or system logs may independently retain technical records. These records are controlled by the relevant device owner, operating-system provider, administrator, or service provider, not by NotifyBridge.

11. Data Retention

The Developer does not maintain a central database containing users’ notification content or pairing information.

Information stored locally by NotifyBridge is retained only while needed to:

* maintain paired-device relationships;
* remember user preferences;
* provide connection and forwarding functionality;
* support notification actions; or
* preserve settings selected by the user.

Temporary notification data is retained only for as long as necessary to process the applicable notification or action, subject to operating-system behavior.

Local configuration and pairing information generally remain on the applicable device until the user:

* removes a paired device;
* resets NotifyBridge;
* clears the application’s storage;
* uninstalls the application; or
* otherwise deletes the applicable data through application or operating-system controls.

12. Data Deletion and User Controls

NotifyBridge does not require an online account. Therefore, there is no server-side NotifyBridge account to delete.

Users can control or delete locally stored NotifyBridge information by:

* disabling notification forwarding;
* removing an individual paired Mac;
* removing all paired devices;
* clearing NotifyBridge application data through Android settings;
* resetting the macOS companion application;
* uninstalling NotifyBridge from Android;
* uninstalling the macOS companion application;
* revoking Notification Access through Android settings;
* revoking camera permission through Android settings;
* revoking notification permission through Android settings;
* removing any battery-optimization exemption; and
* disabling background or startup behavior through device settings where available.

Clearing the Android application’s data or uninstalling it removes data stored in the application’s private Android storage, subject to Android’s system behavior.

Removing or resetting the macOS application removes information stored by that application, subject to macOS system behavior and notification history settings.

Because the Developer does not possess notification content or pairing information through a central NotifyBridge server, the Developer normally has no remotely stored notification or pairing data to retrieve or delete on behalf of the user.

Questions concerning deletion can be submitted using the contact information in Section 20.

13. Security Measures

NotifyBridge is designed to protect communication and pairing information through technical measures appropriate to its architecture.

Depending on the application version and platform capabilities, these measures may include:

* encrypted local-network communication;
* authenticated pairing;
* cryptographic pairing credentials;
* authenticated encryption;
* message-integrity verification;
* TLS certificate validation or certificate pinning;
* Android Keystore-backed key protection;
* replay-protection mechanisms;
* restricted application storage;
* explicit user pairing; and
* rejection of unauthenticated or invalid connection attempts.

These measures are intended to reduce the risk of unauthorized interception, modification, or connection.

However, no software, device, wireless network, encryption method, or transmission method can be guaranteed to be completely secure. Users should:

* install NotifyBridge only from official sources;
* keep Android, macOS, and NotifyBridge updated;
* protect their devices with passwords or biometric authentication;
* use a trusted local network;
* avoid untrusted or public networks;
* remove devices they no longer use;
* protect physical access to the paired Mac; and
* revoke Notification Access when NotifyBridge is no longer needed.

14. Permissions Used by NotifyBridge

Depending on the Android version and enabled functionality, NotifyBridge may request or use system permissions and special access for the following purposes:

Notification Access

Used to read and process eligible Android notifications so they can be forwarded to the user’s paired Mac.

Camera

Used when the user chooses to scan a pairing QR code.

Notifications

Used to display NotifyBridge service status, connection information, and other application notifications.

Foreground Service

Used to maintain the active notification-forwarding service and provide a persistent user-visible indication while the service is running.

Foreground Service Data Synchronization

Used for the continuous transfer and synchronization required by the core device-to-device notification-forwarding functionality.

Battery Optimization Exemption

Used, when enabled by the user, to reduce background restrictions that could interrupt notification forwarding.

Receive Boot Completed

Used, where enabled and supported, to restore the user’s selected service state after the device restarts.

Network Access and Network State

Used to discover and communicate with the paired Mac over the user’s network and to determine connection availability.

Permissions and special access can be reviewed or revoked through Android system settings. Revoking a permission may prevent the related feature from functioning.

15. Local Network Security and User Responsibilities

NotifyBridge is intended for use on a network trusted by the user.

The user is responsible for:

* choosing which Mac to pair;
* ensuring the pairing QR code or credentials are not exposed to unauthorized persons;
* securing the local Wi-Fi or Ethernet network;
* protecting access to the Android device and Mac;
* removing old or unrecognized paired devices; and
* controlling which applications are eligible for forwarding.

Network administrators, employers, schools, public Wi-Fi operators, VPN providers, firewall products, or other network intermediaries may have visibility into network metadata even when the content is encrypted.

NotifyBridge cannot control the security configuration of the user’s router, network, Android device, Mac, or third-party software.
